Shortcut

CSV Import

Got a lot of holdings to update? Shelly can read CSV exports from most UK brokers — or you can use his own template to update everything in one go. This is optional — if you've already updated your numbers above, you're all set.

Where to find your CSV:

Shelly's template (recommended)

The easiest way — especially if you use multiple providers or your broker doesn't offer CSV export. Download the template, fill in your holdings from each app, and import it here. You can list holdings from all your accounts in one file — Shelly matches each row to your existing holdings by ticker or name, regardless of which account they belong to. Keep the file saved and just update the numbers each month. Columns: name, ticker, units, price, value.

Trading 212

App: Menu → History → tap the Export button → select your date range (cover the full month so all buys and sells are captured) → choose which data to include (orders, transactions) → download as CSV. Website: same flow via the History section. Note: max 365 days per export. Shelly reads: Action, Ticker, Name, No. of shares, Price / share, Time.

InvestEngine

Log in on the website (not available in the mobile app). Go to the Reports tab at the top → Create report → select Valuation statement → choose CSV format → pick the date and account → Continue. This gives you a snapshot of your current holdings and their values. Shelly reads: ISIN, Description, Amount and will match them to your existing holdings.

Vanguard Investor

Log in on the website → My Portfolio → look for a Download or Export option. Vanguard UK's export options are limited — if you can't find a CSV download, use Shelly's template and copy the values from your portfolio screen instead. Shelly looks for: Investment name, Sedol/ISIN, Units, Price, Value.

Hargreaves Lansdown

Log in on the website → My Accounts → select your account → look for Statements, valuations and pension illustrations → select an investment report → download as CSV. Note: HL exports cover transactions in 3-month blocks, not a live portfolio snapshot — and some fees may be missing from the CSV. If the export doesn't include your current units and prices, use Shelly's template instead. Shelly looks for: Stock, Sedol, Units held, Price, Value.

AJ Bell

Log in on the website → My Account → Portfolio → look for an Export or Download option. If no CSV is available for your current holdings, use Shelly's template. Shelly looks for: Investment, SEDOL, Quantity, Price, Value.

Freetrade

App: Activity tab → tap the download icon (↑) in the top right → select All Activity → export as CSV. Website: Profile → Settings → Export Data → download. This is a transaction history, so Shelly totals up buys and sells to work out your current position. Note: corporate actions (e.g. stock takeovers) may not be included. Columns: Title, Ticker, Type, Quantity, Price per share.

Interactive Investor

Interactive Investor does not currently offer a CSV export of your portfolio. The best approach is to use Shelly's template — copy your holding names, units and prices from the ii website into the template and import it here. If ii adds a CSV export in future, Shelly looks for: Holding, EPIC/Ticker, Quantity, Price, Value.

Other (generic CSV)

Shelly can read any CSV with sensible column headers. He looks for columns named things like: ticker (or symbol/sedol/isin), name (or holding/investment), units (or quantity/shares), price, and optionally value. Headers are case-insensitive. Or just use the template.
